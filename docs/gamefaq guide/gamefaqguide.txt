System. Everything in the guide is laid out pretty straightforward in the 
table of contents. As of right now, the prices listed in the FAQ are for the 
Fighter class (I will eventually add the prices for the Magician and Saint 
classes). If you have any questions, go check out my e-mail address in 
the "Contact Me" section. And without further ado, I present to you my first 
ever FAQ. Let's start now!!!

*  1. The Story  *
******************

   Here's some of the text from the opening sequence of the game. It is a 
surprisingly good translation for a game made in 1990 that came from a 
virtually unknown Japanese game studio that rarely made games for us North 
American gamers (and yes, that was a great run-on sentence):

   "One day peaceful Arabia was attacked by the demons. The evil magician 
Sabaron summoned them from the dark world for his evil desire. One brave 
magician attempted to fight him. But he failed, and his sweetheart was taken 
and all his memories erased. He was even thrown into a different time period. 
Do you remember this? The young magician is you. Only you can save Arabia. 
Please fight Sabaron again!"

   So basically, you (Isfa) tried to kill Sabaron, but you got majorly pwned 
and Sabaron gave you a bad case of amnesia. You wake up on the other side of 
Arabia (the side with lots of grass, in case you are wondering) with a pocket 
full of change and a couple of extra lives. Coronya the fairy comes over to 
you and says that you must rescue your girlfriend Scheherazade, her three hot 
princess sisters, her father, and then you have to save the world. Of course, 
she doesn't tell you that will have to fight through five monster-filled 
worlds just to get your woman back. Luckily, you will have plenty of allies 
join you on your quest, including a pair of genies, a wooden pumpkin doll, a 
talking robot, a drum-playing rain god, and a monkey thief. Oh joy!

*******************
*  2. Characters  *
*******************

A. ISFA - The main hero. You can name him at the start of the game.

B. CORONYA - Your guide for this quest. She's with you at the very beginning 
of the game, so don't worry about having to find her. Her attacks are Rod, 
Defenee, and Mymy.

C. FARUK - Joins your party in Chapter 1. He can be found in the town of 
Horen in the past. His attacks are Fight and Gilzade. When he uses Fight, he 
will attack twice in one turn.

D. KEBABU - Joins your party in Chapter 1. She can be found in the underwater 
town of Horen in the present. Her attacks are Arrow, Bolttor1, and Seal.

E. GUN MECA - Joins your party in Chapter 2. You'll meet up with him in 
Copanes in the present after you talk to Lah in the town of Alart in the 
past. His attacks are Fight, Bolttor2, and Silleit.

F. SUPICA - Joins your party in Chapter 2. He is found in the Alalart Maze. 
You have to talk to Lah after getting Gun Meca before he will to join you. 
His attacks are Arrow and Seal.

G. EPIN - Joins your party in Chapter 2. He is found in the Dark Palace. His 
attacks are Fight, Defenee, and Whistle.

H. PUKIN - Joins your party in Chapter 3. Supapa makes him for you after you 
get the Cimaron fruit from the Cimaron tree. His attacks are Arrow, Gilzade, 
and Velver.

I. MUSTAFA - Joins your party in Chapter 3. He is in the town of Passora in 
the present. You have to give him 100 Rupias for him to come with you. His 
attacks are Fight, Mymy, and Bolttor2.

J. GUBIBI - Joins your party in Chapter 4. He is in the Yufla Palace in the 
present. His attacks are Rod, Defenee, and Resealo.

K. RAINY - Joins your party in Chapter 4. He is in the underwater town of 
Lava in the past. His attacks are Rod, Perius, and Matato.

L. HASSAN - Joins your party in Chapter 5. He is found in the town of Yufla 
in the present. His attacks are Fight, Flamol3, and Caraba.

*  3. Controls   *
******************

          |A|
                  -Select options on the menus and subscreens.
                  -Use whatever item you set to the A button.
     OPRIN        -Allows you to make a staircase appear in some screens.
     JUMP         -Makes you jump!
          |B|
                  -Exit hotels, shops, mosques, etc.
                  -Cancel a selection during a menu-based battle.
                  -Use whatever item you set to the B button.
     ROD          -A magical rod that you can use to shoot at enemies.
     SWORD        -A sword to attack enemies with.
     SPEAK        -Allows you to talk to the townspeople.
     JUMP         -Makes you jump!
        |START|
                  -Choose an option on the title screen.
                  -Pause the game and bring up the subscreen menu.
        |SELECT|
                  -Change between "Start" and "Continue" on the title screen.
                  -Move between some options in menu-based battles.
                  -Pause the game and bring up a status/map subscreen.
        |D-PAD|
                  -Move your character.
                  -Move the cursor in most of the menus.

*  4. Walkthrough  *
********************

   When you turn on the game, you will have the choice of changing the text 
speed (Slow, Normal, Fast). Always put the speed on Fast unless you want to 
waste your time waiting for the text to crawl onto the screen. To start a new 
game, choose "START". To enter a password from the last time you played, 
chooose "CONTINUE". After you begin, you will be asked to enter a name with 
one to four letters. After that, the story will begin to unfold. When Coronya 
asks for your help, answer "YES" (duh!). You will then be able to select your 
Arabian class (don't worry- you can change your class in the game anytime you 
want). I recommend that you choose the Fighter class to begin with.

     FIGHTER: Your sword is the strongest weapon. Useful for regular fighting.
     SAINT: Your sword and rod are weak. Lets you obtain Pukin in Chapter 3.
     MAGICIAN: Your rod is the strongest weapon. Mostly used for bosses.

   You begin the game with 50 Rupias and 2 extra lives. After you choose your 
class, you will take your first trip through the time door and Chapter 1 will 
begin.....

   After listening to the priest's rantings, you'll begin in the town of 
Meshudo. There is not much to do here, but you should at least visit the shop 
before you leave. Pick up two or three bread loaves and two or three magic 
carpets (the first listed price is the normal price, and the second one is 
the discounted price; always ask for the discount if more than one price is 
listed in the guide. Be careful- if you ask for too many discounts on one 
item, the shopkeeper will take 10 Rupias and throw you out of the store.) You 
can also borrow and repay money at the shop, but you don't need to do this. 
Just ignore this function. You might also want to get acquainted with the how 
hotels and mosques work. Staying the night at a hotel refills your HP and MP. 
At a mosque, you can pay 20 Rupias to bring an ally back to life, you can ask 
for a password, or you can change your class to any of the following:

     Changing your class...
                 to SAINT:     40 Rupias
                 to FIGHTER:   60 Rupias

   You will find the first Magic University in Rudoria. Go there first and 
pass all the classes and collect the upgrades for the sword and the rod. If 
you fail the guy's exam (which is impossible if you have any basic reading 
ability), you can pay 10 Rupias to take it again. If you fail twice (which

pirate dude with 20 Rupias and he'll automatically pass you. Wheee! Once you 
are done at the university, you might want to stop by the shop if you are 
running low on carpets or bread. Now you can begin the long trek to the town 
of Horen.

sells them for the same price (100 Rupias for 4 of them). In the menu-based 
battles, as many as four troopers will fight for you at the start of the

magic attack. They have weak defense, so you'll probably lose a few of them 
in some of the tougher battles. You can carry a maximum of 99 troopers at a 
time. After you've done what you need to do, go to the shop and pick up a map 
(and a carpet if you don't have any). Then go talk to Faruk and he'll join 
your party. With your new, much stronger team, head to the Mooroon Maze.

   This first palace is extremely easy. Follow the path on the map to get to 
the boss. Near the beginning, you'll encounter an enemy that continuously 
spawns from a little "house" in a narrow room. You can destroy its house with 
your sword. Be careful not to fall in the water traps that lie around and 
about. When you get to the room with the two statues, you can either hit one 
of them with a horn to open the door, or you can kill them with your sword.
   When you enter the boss room, 8 eyes will appear. They will slowly open 
and close. To kill them, shoot each one twice with your rod while they are 
wide open. Be careful- the eyes will occasionally use a thunder attack that 
takes away 10 HP per hit. After you defeat the eyes, you will be transported 
to another room. In this room, there are 3 big eyes. They shoot fireballs at 
you. Each eye takes about 10 hits from your rod. Get up close and kill one 
eye at a time. Gilga also uses something called "Stone Magic", but apparently 
this is blocked by that Mirror Shield Kebabu gave to you. After you kill all 
those stupid eyes, Chapter 1 is over ~_~

   The town of Malart is your starting location in this world. Go into town, 
stock up on items, and talk to everyone. This would also be a good time to 
get a password if you are playing on the NES. Your next stop will be the town 
of Copanes, but you need to make a stop at the old man's place first and get

a while. This is a good place to level up a few times, but don't waste your 
time attacking the jumping scorpions- they take too long to kill (you have to 
hit them multiple times before they go back underground). Focus on attacking 
the other enemies you find around and about. Get to around level 7 before you 
enter Copanes.

   Now that you have Gun Meca, go back to Lah in Alart. Answer "NO" and he 
will give you the Magic Boots and then tell you that Supica is locked up in 
the maze. Go south and use OPRIN to enter the Alalart Maze. Go up one screen. 
From here, follow the path until you get to the lower area of the "1" screen. 
There will be a big path heading up. This path is filled with many invisible 
holes. Get on the right side of the path, walk up, and stop when you are 
about two-thirds of the way up the screen (you should be lined up with the 
paths on the left and right side of the screen). Now walk over to the left 
wall and walk up to screen "2". This screen has holes in the exact same

*  6. Items and Spells  *
*************************

   The number next to each item represents how many you can hold, while the 
number next to each spell tells how much MP it takes to use that form of 
magic.

     ITEMS:

->BREAD (10): Automatically restores 50 HP when you lose all of your HP.
->MASHROOB (10): Automatically restores 50 MP when you lose all of your MP.
->CARPET (15): Lets you warp to other cities and escape from palaces and 
mazes. Magic carpets cannot be used in during boss battles.
->R.SEED (5): Abbreviation for Rupia seed. You can plant this in a magic 
field during an Alalart Solar Eclipse, or you can eat it. If you plant it, a 
Rupia Tree will grow and you'll get lots of money. If you eat it, you will 
become invisible for a short period of time.
->MAP (1): A map for the palaces and mazes of a world. You can view it by 
pressing Select. There is one to be found in every world.
->KEY (9): Lets you open locked doors in palaces. It is used automatically.
->HORN (5): A Kibra's Horn. Shoot it at the stone statues in palaces.
->HAMMER (5): Causes stars to fall from the sky. It's HammerTime!
->RING (1): You automatically get this when Kebabu joins your party. It can 
be used during boss battles only. When you use it, Kebabu will carry you to 
the previous room of the palace.
->AMULET (9): When a boss or mini-boss uses a transformation magic, the 
Amulet makes you change back to your normal form faster. It is used 
automatically.

     SPELLS:

->OPRIN (5 MP): Causes a secret stairway to appear on some screens.
->PAMPOO (2 MP): Restores 10 HP.
->BOLTTOR1 (4 MP): A lightning bolt hits one enemy on-screen.
->BOLTTOR2 (15 MP): [Replaces BOLTTOR1]
->BOLTTOR3 (20 MP): [Replaces BOLTTOR2]
->DEFENEE (2 MP): Raises your defense temporarily.
->FLAMOL1 (20 MP): A wave of fire hits all enemies on-screen.
->FLAMOL2 (25 MP): [Replaces FLAMOL1]
->FLAMOL3 (30 MP): [Replaces FLAMOL2]
->CORBOCK (2 MP): Temporarily changes all enemies on-screen into tiny people.
->SHRINK (2 MP): [Replaces CORBOCK]
->CARABA (20 MP): [Replaces SHRINK]
->RAMIPAS (10 MP): You won't encounter enemies for a while.
->MARITA (4 MP): Restores 50 HP.
->MONECOM: Monecom can be used in any world during an Alalart Solar Eclipse, 
but you can only use it once in the game. It gives you 999 Rupias and the 
maximum amount of each item.
->RAINCOM: Raincom can be used in world 2 or 4 during an Alalart Solar 
Eclipse, but once you use it you can never use it again. It stops you from 
taking damage caused by the desert and makes grass grow over the sand. It 
also fills up your HP and ends the current solar eclipse. Plus, it stops 
random on-screen enemy encounters (but there are still random RPG battles).
->SPRICOM: Spricom can only be used in the future of world 3 during an 
Alalart Solar Eclipse, and it can only be used once. It stops you from taking 
damage in the snow and causes winter to change into spring. It also fills up 
your HP and ends the current solar eclipse. Plus, it stops random on-screen 
enemy encounters (but there are still random RPG battles).
->MOSCOM: Moscom can be used in world 4 or 5 during an Alalart Solar Eclipse. 
It allows one-time instant access to a mosque, but this mosque can only be 
used to change your class. But, this service is free. This is very useful if 
you need to change your class before you fight Salamander.
->LIBCOM: Libcom can only be used in world 5 during an Alalart Solar Eclipse. 
It brings all your allies back to life and fills everyone's HP and MP.

*  7. Magic Field and Magic Star Locations  *
*********************************************

MAGIC FIELDS:
   In The Magic of Scheherazade, there are secret areas called magic fields. 
They are usually accessed by using the magic spell, OPRIN (not to be confused 
with Oprah- Oprah! Oprah! Come in, Oprah!). During an Alalart Solar Eclipse, 
you can plant a Rupia seed at these places. When you go back to the area in 
the future, you can harvest the Rupias from the Rupia tree. Here are the 
planting and harvesting magic field locations:

   CHAPTER 1: Planting area is 1E of Rudoria (past).
              Harvest area is 1E of Rudoria (present).
   CHAPTER 2: Planting area is 1W and 5S of Alart (past).
              Harvest area is 1W and 3S of Copanes (present).
   CHAPTER 3: Planting area is 1S and 1W of Passora (present).
              Harvest area is 1W of Kasimeel (future).
   CHAPTER 4: Planting area is 3W, 2N, 2E, 1N, and 1W of Farvil (past).
              Harvest area is 2W, 1N, 1E, 1N, and 1E of Chigris (present).
   CHAPTER 5: --None--

MAGIC STARS:
   In this game, there are mysterious gold screens that have a picture of a 
star in the center. I believe they are hexagrams. I will call these "Magic 
Stars" (ohh, I'm getting imaginative now). If you stand on the star, your MP 
is completely refilled. This is an excellent way to refill HP, too. Just use 
PAMPOO or MARITA to fill up your HP, and then fill up your MP by stepping on 
the star. You can fill your MP as many times as you want- just leave the 
screen and go back to star.

   CHAPTER 1: --None--
   CHAPTER 2: In the past, go 1E and 1N of the time door.
   CHAPTER 3: In the present, go 2S, 1W, 2S, 2W, 1S, 1W, and 1N of Kasimeel.
   CHAPTER 4: In the past, go 1S, 2W, and 2N of Yufla Palace.
   CHAPTER 5: In the present, you can find it in Sabaron's Palace.
